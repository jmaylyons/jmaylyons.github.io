Williams, a strong voice in community, determined to make a difference

After Rosa Parks sat, Rosa Williams walked. It was the late 1950s, back when blacks and whites in Gainesville had different water fountains, different restaurants, different schools. African-Americans went to the Negro library on Northwest First Street, where the shelves were stocked with books with missing pages and coloring in the margins.
