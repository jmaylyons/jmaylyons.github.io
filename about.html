About

I was born in Orlando, Fla., but grew up 20 minutes north in Apopka, a town most notable for its World Series finalist Little League team, indoor foliage festival and overly polluted lake. I don't Bible thump, but my beliefs can be summed up by Romans 8:38-39. My mom was a country girl, born and raised in St. Thomas, Jamaica. My father was a city boy who grew up in Fort Wayne, Indiana. They met as students at Tuskegee Institute, and now years later, my sisters and I term ourselves "Jamerican." As for Journalism, I ended up here through a series of fortunate events. It started my sophomore year in high school when my sister recommended I write for the student newspaper. Two years later, I was accepted to the Univerity of Florida and decided to major in journalism. I grew up viewing journalism as more impersonal, cold.
But I was wrong.
I thank the art of storytelling for that. My freshman year at UF I was introduced to true storytelling. I devoured page after page of Rick Bragg's "Somebody Told Me," and I realized I knew what I wanted to do for the rest of my life.
Now, in my junior year at the j-school I've been able to immerse myself in the field. I spend two nights a week working from 6 to past midnight at the Independent Florida Alligator copy desk, and I spend two days a week at the Gainesville Sun, where I shadow city reporter Chris Curry.
