Published Work

I'll never forget my first story for The Independent Florida Alligator. It had to do with a special program the astronomy department was hosting for high school students.
It was horrible.
I was told it was published online, but I think they were just being nice. I'll also never forget the last story I wrote for the Alligator before I was hired as a copy editor. I was looking for any event to cover for Reporting two summers ago, and I stumbled upon a coupon clipping class. Little did I know, it would make quite the story. I sent it to the Alligator, and while I was there editing, one of the editors remembered my name from the copy editing test. She asked me if I would be interested in working there.
I said yes.
Since then, I've come a long way, and I have gotten to cover topics ranging from something as serious as the utility issues in Gainesville to more lighthearted topics—like a security guard at a retirement home who takes residents' coupons from the trash.
